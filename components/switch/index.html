Overview

Switch is a control that is used to quickly switch between two possible states. Toggles are only used for these binary actions that occur immediately after the user "flips the switch". They are commonly used for "on/off" switches.

When to use

  • Toggle a single item on or off
  • Immediately activate or deactivate something

Anatomy

Label text

Use labels with a toggle so the action is clear. Labels should be three words or less and appear on the side of a toggle.

You only could use a switch without a label when it is placed in a data grid. In this case the label will be the table header column.

Track

Change background color when is checked (primary color) or unchecked (white color)

Thumb

Change background color when is checked (primary color) or unchecked (white color)

Placement

Switches are often arranged in stacked layouts, otherwise they can also be used independently either in status bars, cards or to activate and deactivate entire sections in complex forms.

Behaviour

A switch is successfully toggled when the switch thumb slides to the other side of the track upon user interaction.

When a user toggles a switch, its thumb size changes and the corresponding action takes effect immediately.
