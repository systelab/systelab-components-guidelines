Typography

Typography creates purposeful texture, guiding users to read and understand the hierarchy of information. The right typographic treatment and the controlled usage of type styles helps manage the display of content, keeping it useful, simple, and effective.

Overview

Systelab Components uses Lato type face for all visual interfaces.

Lato is a sans-serif, Humanist typeface. Humanist typefaces characters have the greatest line width variations and the most contrast in shape. They rank better in ease and speed of reading among all types of sans-serif styles.

Lato font family is available at Google Fonts.

Weights

Regular (400)

Light (300)

Font weight is an important typographic variable that can add emphasis and differentiate content hierarchy. Font weight and size pairings must be carefully balanced. A bold weight will always have more emphasis than a lighter weight font of the same size. However, a lighter weight font can rank hierarchically higher than a bold font if the lighter weight type size is significantly larger than the bold one.

Usage

In order to maintain the hierarchy within the application we must try to combine the different sizes available to ensure that the user does not get lost, especially in large information screens. Here are some examples of how to apply the different styles.

Category Use
H1, H2, H3,H4,H5,H6,
In the type scale, headlines span from a range of 1 through 6. Headlines are the largest text on the screen, reserved for short, important text or numerals. These unconventional font designs have details and intricacy that help attract the eye.
Subtitle
Subtitles are smaller than headlines. They are typically reserved for medium-emphasis text that is shorter in length.
  • Card titles
  • Title page in applications(MyPatients)
Body 1
Body text in range 1 is used in most of the application's text.
  • Form elements: label, inputs, buttons, tabs
  • As a value in a title/value component
Body 2
It is applied in texts where we have little space or as content separators to complement information and being smaller helps to hierarchize the information when used with other types of content.
  • Menu with icon + text
  • Indicate status
  • As content separator title
  • As a text/value component in small spaces likes sidebar, statusbar
Caption
Caption and overline text are the smallest font sizes. They are used sparingly to annotate imagery, introduce a headline or a text/value component.
  • To indicate standby status, eg: Loading..., Processing...
Overline
Caption and overline text are the smallest font sizes. They are used sparingly to annotate imagery, introduce a headline or a text/value component.
  • In badges, most often to indicate states
  • As a title in a title/value component
You can combine the weights of the same type to highlight information.
